Summary

Calendar Description: This course will provide a conceptual and practical understanding of how to leverage technologies in a range of common activities such as searching, social networking, presenting, and creating web content. The emphasis will be on using and understanding digital technologies in effective and ethical ways in our digital society. This is an undergraduate course that is a mix of lecture, discussion, tutorial and workshop.

This instance of the course (Fall 2014) will focus on interactive, web-based visualization of information. Considerable time and effort will be devoted to learning how to code (HTML, CSS, Javascript…). You need not have any technical background or programming experience, but you do need to be thoroughly committed to learning new skills that will require abundant perseverance and resourcefulness. The most important skills you will learn are not coding competencies, they're problem-solving competencies: how to figure out how to do what you don't know how to do in the process of creating digital works.

Despite the focus on web-based development, this is not a programming course, it is a course about effective communication in a digital society. We are motivated by a desire to combine the traditional strengths of the humanities – critical thinking, effective expression, historical and cultural perspectives, nuanced and imaginative approaches, etc. –  with technical skills that empower us to become creators of compelling digital content, going beyond more conventional academic forms of expression (like the essay).

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to

  • critically assess data visualizations (from news sources, blogs, advertising, etc.)
  • acquire, parse, filter and mine data
  • create and refine interactive data visualizations
  • develop effective strategies for learning new visualization techniques and solving problems
  • plan and implement a collaborative digital project
  • better understand and discuss the significance of data and data visualizations in our digital society

(Very) Tentative Schedule

  • Week 1: intro to the course; gentle introduction to web authoring
  • Week 2: visual information design; basic HTML
  • Week 3: epistemology of data; basic styling
  • Week 4: big data; web mashups
  • Week 5: aesthetics of data; web developer tools
  • Week 6: copyright; introduction to D3
  • Week 7: privacy; HTML DOM
  • Week 8: ethics; JSON & data
  • Week 9: politics; basic charts in D3
  • Week 10: open data; more charts in D3
  • Week 11: hacking; beyond charts
  • Week 12: journalism; custom visulizations
  • Week 13: student poster session; custom visulizations
